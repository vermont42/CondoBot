# Activities & Things to Do — Kailua-Kona

## Beaches

**Magic Sands Beach** — Popular beach with white sand and great swimming. Ideal for sunbathing and beach games.
Address: 77-6481 Ali'i Dr, Kailua-Kona

**Kukio Beach** — Pristine, less-crowded beach with clear waters, white sand, and excellent snorkeling. Great for a peaceful day.
Address: 72-100 Kaupulehu Drive, Kailua-Kona

**Kaimu Black Sand Beach** — Striking black sand formed by recent lava flows. Unique spot for photos and volcanic scenery.
Address: 12-5032 Pahoa Kalapana Rd, Pāhoa

**Two Step Beach at Honaunau Bay** — Excellent snorkeling and swimming with clear waters and abundant marine life.
Address: 84-5571 Honaunau Beach Road, Captain Cook

## Hikes

**Pololu Valley Lookout and Trail** — Scenic lookout with breathtaking views of a lush valley and coastline. Trail leads to a black sand beach; moderately challenging.
Address: 52-5100 Akoni Pule Highway, Kapaau

**Captain Cook Monument Trail** — Historic and scenic hike leading to the Captain Cook Monument with beautiful coastal views.

**Kīlauea Iki Trail** — In Volcanoes National Park. Hike across a volcanic crater floor with stunning lava formations and tropical rainforest views.

**Mauna Kea Summit Hike** — Challenging hike with panoramic island views from the top of the world's tallest volcano.

## Family-Friendly Activities

**Pana'ewa Zoo** — Home to 82 species and nearly 300 animals in a rainforest setting. Features playground, pavilions, and gift shop.

**Sea Turtle Watching at Punalu'u Beach** — See sea turtles basking on black sand. All-ages activity with educational opportunities.

**Kona Ocean Adventures** — Family-friendly boat tours for snorkeling, whale watching, or dolphin watching. Educational and enjoyable for all ages.

**Atlantis Submarines** — Underwater adventure aboard a real submarine exploring marine life and underwater landscapes.

**Ali'i Gardens Marketplace** — Local artisan market with 20+ unique shops. Free parking.

**Hawaii Volcanoes National Park** — Volcanic craters, lava tubes, geothermal features, family-friendly trails, and educational exhibits.

## Cultural and Educational

**Pu'uhonua o Honaunau National Historical Park** — Ancient Hawaiian historic site with a preserved "Place of Refuge" and cultural demonstrations.

**Ahu'ena Heiau** — Restored Hawaiian temple (heiau) providing insight into ancient Hawaiian religious practices. Frequented by Kamehameha the Great.

**Mokuaikaua Church** — Hawaii's oldest Christian church with beautiful architecture and significant cultural heritage.

**Mauna Kea Observatory** — World-class astronomical observatory for stargazing and educational programs. Tip: Requires a four-wheel-drive vehicle to reach the summit; the road sometimes closes for weather. Call ahead.

## Adventure and Water Sports

**Manta Ray Night Dive** — Unforgettable night dive seeing manta rays up close.

**Snorkeling at Kealakekua Bay** — Vibrant marine life and coral reefs in a protected bay. Kona Ocean Adventures offers trips there.

**Kayaking and Stand-Up Paddleboarding** — Rent equipment or book guided tours to explore the Kona coastline and calm waters.

**Ziplining Adventures** — Zipline through lush Hawaiian landscape via various tour operators.

## Agricultural Tourism

**Kona Coffee Farm Tours** — Visit local coffee farms for tastings and interactive experiences. Recommended: Kona Coffee Living History Farm, Rooster Farms, and Mountain Thunder Kona Coffee.

**Chocolate Farm Tours** — Visit cacao farms for tastings and tours. Recommended: Kuaiwi Farm, Original Hawaiian Chocolate Factory, and Honoka'a Chocolate Company.

**Kanaloa Octopus Research Center** — Guided tours and hands-on exhibits about octopus farming and these intelligent creatures.

**Ocean Rider Sea Horse Farm** — Learn about seahorse breeding and conservation through an interactive, educational experience.

**Macadamia Nut Farm Tours** — Recommended: O.K. Farms, which grows macadamia and other crops in a spectacular natural setting.

## Golf Courses

**Kona Country Club** — 18-hole course with stunning ocean views in Keauhou. Several holes along the coastline. Good for beginners and experienced golfers.

**Makalei Golf Club** — On the slopes of Hualālai with panoramic views. 18 holes surrounded by tropical forests with varied elevations.

**Mauna Lani Golf Courses** — Two 18-hole courses (North and South). The South Course has an iconic 15th hole set against dramatic ocean cliffs.

**Hualālai Golf Course** — Jack Nicklaus-designed course at the Four Seasons Resort. Oceanfront holes with luxurious service.

**Waikoloa Beach Resort Golf** — Two courses: the Beach Course (oceanfront holes) and the Kings' Course (links-style with views of ancient lava flows).

**Makani Golf Club** — Higher elevation with cooler temperatures. Known for lakes, waterfalls, and abundant wildlife.

## Horseback Riding

**Parker Ranch** (Waimea) — One of the oldest/largest cattle ranches in the U.S. Guided tours through rolling pastures with views of Mauna Kea. Experience paniolo (Hawaiian cowboy) culture.

**Dahana Ranch** (Waimea) — Personalized rides through open pastures with mountain and coastline views. Tailored to all skill levels.

**Paniolo Adventures** (Kohala Coast) — Beginner to advanced treks through ranchlands with panoramic ocean and mountain views.

**Na'alapa Stables** (Waipio Valley) — Ride through tropical forests, cross streams, and discover hidden waterfalls with knowledgeable guides.

## Local Festivals and Events

**Kona Coffee Cultural Festival** — Annually in November. Tastings, parades, and cultural events celebrating Kona coffee.

**Ironman World Championship** — Each October. Famous triathlon with stunning views and energetic atmosphere.

**Kona Brewers Festival** — In March. Craft beers from local and regional breweries, plus live music and food.

**Hawaiian Slack Key Guitar Festival** — In January. Performances celebrating this unique Hawaiian musical tradition.

**King Kamehameha Day** — June 11th annually. Vibrant parade, hula performances, and lei-draping ceremony honoring Kamehameha I.

**Merrie Monarch Festival** — In April in Hilo. World-class hula performances and competitions for men and women.
